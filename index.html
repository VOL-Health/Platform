Choose your VOL Health™ view

Use VOL Health as a single framework, then pick the lens that fits the conversation: volatility today, or workforce stability over time.

VOL Health™ Volatility Index (VI)
Directional diagnostic for volatility, operations, and labor strain.
›
Directional diagnostic that scores the Trinity of Volatility, Operations & Labor across ten drivers — census, OT, PBJ exposure, survey risk, reimbursement, reputation, leadership bandwidth, and more.
  • Why VI: Make endemic volatility visible in one stability score and economic range.
  • How it works: Ten inputs roll into a 0–100 stability score and directional annual economics.
  • Details: Calibrated for PBJ, OT, survey risk, and portfolio views.
View Volatility Index →
Workforce Stability Index (WSI)
Companion view focused on workforce pipeline stability and drag.
›
Scores staffing volatility, financial exposure, operational & compliance risk, and workforce pipeline health into a single WSI score.
  • Why WSI: Quantify how unstable schedules, vacancies, and turnover drag operations and margin.
  • How it works: Mix of direct inputs or portfolio distributions roll up into a 0–100 WSI.
  • Details: Shows volatility cost, stabilizable upside, and workforce drag as dollar ranges.

SHAI™ Stability Engine
Activation layer that reduces near-term volatility while strengthening long-term workforce stability.
›
SHAI connects insight to action:
  • SHAI Buffer: Improves shift continuity and reliability to reduce near-term volatility.
  • SHAI Workforce Engine: Identifies high-fit internal hire candidates and pipeline actions.
  • Stability Delta: Tracks improvement over time: ΔVI and ΔWSI.

VOL Health Stability Map

A portfolio-friendly view that plots each community by WSI on the horizontal axis and VI on the vertical axis. The goal is simple: move north-east over time and measure the Stability Delta.

Stability Zones
How to interpret the map
Acute Volatility
instability is active and compounding
Operational Stability
strong workforce and controlled operations
Structural Instability
fragile workforce foundation beneath the surface
Managed Volatility
pressure is present, but being contained

How communities move north-east

  • WSI increases to the right as workforce resilience, coverage reliability, and pipeline health improve.
  • VI increases upward as operational volatility becomes more controlled and manageable.
  • Stability Delta is the movement from one point in time to the next: ΔWSI and ΔVI.
